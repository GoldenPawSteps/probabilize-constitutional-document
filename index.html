IBalance

Every citizen of Probabilize begins with:

Founding Endowment
$$B = 1$$

Balance is your economic capacity — the fuel you spend to participate in markets. It is not merely wealth; it is the measure of your ability to commit capital to belief. You cannot spend what you do not have. No exceptions.

IIMarkets

A market is a formal, public commitment to a question. It is the atomic unit of epistemic life in Probabilize. There are three operations: Make, Take, and Unmake.

Make — Creating a Market

A citizen who believes a question is worth resolving creates a market by specifying:

  • Outcomes: \(\omega = (\omega_1, \ldots, \omega_n)\) — a finite, exhaustive set of mutually exclusive possibilities
  • Prior probabilities: \(p = (p_1, \ldots, p_n)\), where every \(p_i > 0\) and \(\sum_i p_i = 1\)
  • Liquidity parameter: \(b > 0\) — governing how responsive prices are to new capital

The maker is not merely asking a question. They are capitalizing it. The required liquidity deposit is:

$$L = -b \cdot \ln(\min_i \, p_i)$$

This is the worst-case subsidy the maker guarantees to pay. The market may only be created if the maker can afford it: \(B_m \geq L\). Upon creation, the maker's balance updates: \(B_m \leftarrow B_m - L\).

The cost function governing all market transactions is:

LMSR Cost Function
$$C(\mathbf{q}) = L + b \cdot \ln\!\left(\sum_i p_i \cdot e^{q_i / b}\right)$$

where \(\mathbf{q} = (q_1, \ldots, q_n)\) tracks the cumulative quantity vector of all positions taken. At initialization, \(\mathbf{q} = \mathbf{0}\), and \(C(\mathbf{0}) = L + b \cdot \ln(1) = L\). The market opens at exactly the cost the maker paid to create it.

Take — Buying a Position

Any citizen except the maker may take positions in an open market. A taker selects a vector \(\Delta\mathbf{q} = (\Delta q_1, \ldots, \Delta q_n)\) representing how much of each outcome they wish to hold. Two constraints must hold:

1. Balance constraint:

$$B_t \geq \Delta C, \quad \text{where} \quad \Delta C = C(\mathbf{q}_m + \Delta\mathbf{q}) - C(\mathbf{q}_m)$$

The taker pays the marginal cost of moving the market from its current state to the new state. They cannot commit more than they own.

2. Nonnegative holdings:

$$\mathbf{q}_t + \Delta\mathbf{q} \geq \mathbf{0} \quad \text{(component-wise)}$$

A taker cannot hold a negative position in any outcome — you cannot owe the market. Upon a valid take: \(\mathbf{q}_m \leftarrow \mathbf{q}_m + \Delta\mathbf{q}\), \(\mathbf{q}_t \leftarrow \mathbf{q}_t + \Delta\mathbf{q}\), \(B_t \leftarrow B_t - \Delta C\).

Unmake — Closing a Market

Only the maker may close a market. Upon unmake, all positions are liquidated and capital is distributed according to the market-implied probabilities at the time of closure.

The gradient of \(C\) is:

$$\frac{\partial C}{\partial q_i} = \frac{p_i \cdot e^{q_i / b}}{\sum_j p_j \cdot e^{q_j / b}}$$

These gradient components sum to 1 and represent the market-implied probability of each outcome — this is how the market speaks.

The maker receives:

$$B_m \leftarrow B_m + C(\mathbf{q}_m) - \nabla C(\mathbf{q}_m) \cdot \mathbf{q}_m$$

Each taker receives:

$$B_t \leftarrow B_t + \nabla C(\mathbf{q}_m) \cdot \mathbf{q}_t$$
Unmake is settlement at current prices, not reimbursement at cost. What each participant receives is not what they originally paid. The operation converts each participant's influence to balance individually and simultaneously, at the uniform rate \(\nabla C(\mathbf{q}_m)\). For every participant, \(\Delta B_i = +I_i\) and \(\Delta I_i = -I_i\), so \(\Delta R_i = 0\) exactly. The total influence across the market reaches zero as a consequence of these individual conversions — because no participant retains any influence in a closed market, and no influence is left over. This is not retirement or destruction; it is the residue of perfect individual conservation applied simultaneously to all.

For a taker who acquired position \(\mathbf{q}_t\) at market state \(\mathbf{q}_{\text{old}}\), paying \(\Delta C = C(\mathbf{q}_{\text{new}}) - C(\mathbf{q}_{\text{old}})\), the discrepancy between payment and receipt decomposes precisely along temporal lines: it is zero for all market activity before the taker's entry (because they held no position), generated partially at their own Take, and completed by every subsequent trade that repriced their position. Unmake does not create this discrepancy, correct it, or distribute it between participants. It crystallizes it, converting whatever the final tally happens to be from influence into balance.

A taker's net relevance gain or loss is determined entirely by what happens from the moment they take a position forward — their own trade, and every subsequent trade that reprices their stake. The market's past is not their inheritance.

The market is settled and ceases to exist.

IIIInfluence

While a market is active, capital is not sitting idle in balances — it is deployed into the market structure. Influence measures the value of your deployed capital across all open markets.

For a maker of market \(m\):

$$I_m^{\text{maker}} = C(\mathbf{q}_m) - \nabla C(\mathbf{q}_m) \cdot \mathbf{q}_m$$

For a taker in market \(m\):

$$I_t^{\text{taker}} = \nabla C(\mathbf{q}_m) \cdot \mathbf{q}_t$$

Total influence is the sum across all active markets in which a person participates. Influence is dynamic — it fluctuates as others take positions and shift the implied probabilities. A taker whose outcome becomes more probable sees their influence rise. A taker on a less favored outcome sees it fall. This fluctuation is not noise. It is the continuous repricing of committed belief by the market, and it begins the moment a position is acquired — not before.

IVRelevance — The Conserved Quantity

The most fundamental law in Probabilize is the conservation of Relevance:

Conservation Law
$$R = B + I$$

Relevance is the sum of balance and influence. To state the conservation law with full precision requires a careful taxonomy of the three operations — because they differ not only in their economic content but in their scope, their effect on individual relevance, and their relationship to each participant's history.

Make is personal in both scope and effect. Only the maker is involved. The maker pays \(L\) from balance and gains exactly \(L\) in influence. No other participant exists in this operation.

$$\Delta R_m = 0 \quad \text{(maker only; others untouched)}$$

Unmake is social in scope but personal in effect. The maker initiates it unilaterally, but it converts the influence of every participant simultaneously — each individually, at the uniform rate \(\nabla C(\mathbf{q}_m)\). For every participant \(i\): \(\Delta B_i = +I_i\), \(\Delta I_i = -I_i\), \(\Delta R_i = 0\). No relevance moves between participants. The total influence across the market reaches zero as a consequence of these individual conversions — not as an independent event, but as what remains when every participant's influence has been exactly converted to balance and nothing is left to hold.

This personal character of Unmake has a precise temporal basis. Before a taker enters the market, their position is \(\mathbf{q}_t = \mathbf{0}\). Every Take that occurred before their entry shifted \(\nabla C\) — but applied to a zero position, all such shifts leave them completely unaffected. Exposure begins at the moment they commit capital and ends at Unmake. The market's prior history is constitutionally irrelevant to their settlement.

Take is social in both scope and effect. A trade reprices the holdings of every participant in the market simultaneously. Individual relevances shift. Their sum does not.

$$\Delta R_i \neq 0 \text{ in general}, \quad \text{but} \quad \sum_i \Delta R_i = 0 \quad \text{always}$$

The lifecycle of a position makes this concrete:

$$\underbrace{\text{Pay } \Delta C}_{\text{Take: balance decreases}} \;\longrightarrow\; \underbrace{\text{Hold } \mathbf{q}_t}_{\substack{\text{influence fluctuates} \\ \text{from entry forward}}} \;\longrightarrow\; \underbrace{\text{Receive } \nabla C(\mathbf{q}_m) \cdot \mathbf{q}_t}_{\text{Unmake: influence converted individually}}$$

Take is the only operation where relevance crosses between participants. Make creates the arena. Unmake dissolves it — converting every participant's influence individually to balance, with the total reaching zero as a consequence of that individual perfection. Take is where the actual epistemic and economic contest occurs.

Total relevance is neither created nor destroyed. The total in the system at any moment equals the total number of citizens — because each person begins with \(R = 1\) and no operation violates conservation at the system level. This is the constitutional guarantee that Probabilize is not an engine of inflation or extraction. No authority can conjure credibility. No participant can acquire influence without surrendering an equal quantity of balance. All value that enters a market must eventually leave it.

VGovernance

Markets in Probabilize are not merely predictive instruments. They are governing mechanisms. Two properties determine a market's governance role:

Market-implied probability — the gradient \(\nabla C(\mathbf{q}_m)\) — defines the probability distribution over outcomes as collectively expressed by all committed capital. This is not a poll. It is not a vote. It is a distribution weighted by skin in the game.

Credibility — \(C(\mathbf{q}_m)\), the current cost of the market — measures how much total capital backs this distribution. A market with higher cost has attracted more committed belief. It speaks with greater authority.

When a governance decision is required, the outcome is sampled randomly according to \(\nabla C(\mathbf{q}_m)\), and its authority is proportional to \(C(\mathbf{q}_m)\).

This is the core epistemology of Probabilize: truth is not declared, it is sampled from a distribution of committed capital. No individual can dictate the outcome. No authority overrides the market. The system governs through probabilistic aggregation of genuine belief.

VIThe Mechanics in Motion — A Worked Example

Abstract principles require concrete instantiation. What follows is a full worked example — every formula evaluated, every balance tracked, every unit of relevance accounted for — demonstrating that the constitutional guarantees are not aspirational but arithmetically necessary.

The Scenario

A binary market is opened on the question: "Does the proposal pass?"

Three citizens. Three units of relevance in the system. That total will not change.

Step 1

Alice Makes the Market

The liquidity requirement is:

$$L = -b \cdot \ln(\min_i\, p_i) = -0.5 \cdot \ln(0.40) = -0.5 \cdot (-0.9163) = \mathbf{0.4581}$$

\(L\) is the maker's maximum possible loss: it grows with \(b\) (deeper liquidity demands more capital) and with the "surprise" of the rarest outcome. At \(b \to 0\), markets become infinitely sharp and cheap; at \(b \to \infty\), infinitely liquid and expensive.

Alice can afford this — \(B_A = 1.0000 \geq 0.4581\) — and the market is created:

$$B_A \leftarrow 1.0000 - 0.4581 = \mathbf{0.5419}$$

The market initializes at \(\mathbf{q} = (0, 0)\), and the cost function confirms it opened at exactly \(L\):

$$C(0,0) = 0.4581 + 0.5 \cdot \ln\!\left(0.6 \cdot e^0 + 0.4 \cdot e^0\right) = 0.4581 + 0.5 \cdot \ln(1) = \mathbf{0.4581}$$

Make is personal in both scope and effect. Alice pays \(L\) from balance and gains \(L\) in influence: \(\Delta B_A = -L\), \(\Delta I_A = +L\), \(\Delta R_A = 0\). Bob and Carol do not exist within this operation.

Step 3

Carol Takes a Position on "No"

Carol acquires 1 unit of \(\omega_2\), committing \(\Delta\mathbf{q} = (0, 1)\) against the current market state \(\mathbf{q}_m = (1, 0)\). The new state becomes \(\mathbf{q}_m = (1, 1)\), and Carol's holdings are \(\mathbf{q}_C = (0, 1)\).

$$C(1,1) = 0.4581 + 0.5 \cdot \ln\!\left(e^2 \cdot (0.6 + 0.4)\right) = 0.4581 + 0.5 \cdot 2 = \mathbf{1.4581}$$

Carol's trade cost: \(\Delta C_C = 1.4581 - 1.2458 = \mathbf{0.2123}\)

Carol spends only \(0.2123\) to acquire 1 unit of \(\omega_2\) — the same quantity Bob acquired of \(\omega_1\) for \(0.7877\). Carol entered at state \((1,0)\) — not at \((0,0)\). The market's prior history, including Bob's distortion to 91.73%, is not her inheritance. She paid \(0.2123\) for a position immediately worth \(\nabla C(1,1) \cdot (0,1) = 0.4000\): her own-Take drift is \(\Delta R_C^{\text{own}} = -0.2123 + 0.4000 = +0.1877\), sourced from Bob, whose position fell from \(0.9173\) to \(0.6000\) as the gradient was restored.

$$B_C \leftarrow 1.0000 - 0.2123 = \mathbf{0.7877}$$

Implied probabilities after Carol's trade:

$$\pi_1 = \frac{0.6 \cdot e^2}{e^2} = \mathbf{0.6000}, \qquad \pi_2 = \frac{0.4 \cdot e^2}{e^2} = \mathbf{0.4000}$$

When both outcome positions are equal, the exponential terms cancel and implied probabilities revert exactly to the priors. Carol's countervailing trade has restored the prior distribution — but at different prices than those at which Bob acted. The market has memory.

Step 4

Alice Unmakes the Market

Alice closes the market at final state \(\mathbf{q}_m = (1,1)\), \(\nabla C = (0.6000,\ 0.4000)\). Each participant's influence is converted individually to balance at this uniform rate: \(\Delta B_i = +I_i\), \(\Delta I_i = -I_i\), \(\Delta R_i = 0\) for every \(i\).

$$B_A \leftarrow 0.5419 + \left[1.4581 - (0.6 + 0.4)\right] = 0.5419 + 0.4581 = \mathbf{1.0000}$$ $$B_B \leftarrow 0.2123 + 0.6000 = \mathbf{0.8123}$$ $$B_C \leftarrow 0.7877 + 0.4000 = \mathbf{1.1877}$$

The total influence across the market reaches zero as a consequence of these individual conversions — because every participant's influence has been exactly converted and nothing remains. This is not retirement; it is the residue of individual conservation applied simultaneously to all.

$$\text{Total: } 1.0000 + 0.8123 + 1.1877 = \mathbf{3.0000} \checkmark$$

Path-Dependent Pricing

The cost of a trade is not a fixed property of the outcome. It is a function of the current market state, which encodes the full history of prior trades. The correct demonstration requires two identical trades — same outcome, same quantity — executed at different market states.

First unit of Pass — at state \((0, 0)\):

$$\Delta C^{(1)}_{\omega_1} = C(1,0) - C(0,0) = 1.4394 - 0.9163 = \mathbf{0.5231}$$

Second unit of Pass — at state \((1, 0)\):

$$C(2, 0) = 0.9163 + \ln\!\left(0.40 \cdot e^{2} + 0.60 \cdot e^{0}\right) = 0.9163 + \ln(3.5556) = 0.9163 + 1.2685 = \mathbf{2.1848}$$ $$\Delta C^{(2)}_{\omega_1} = C(2,0) - C(1,0) = 2.1848 - 1.4394 = \mathbf{0.7454}$$

Same outcome. Same quantity. The second unit costs 42.5% more than the first.

A trader who arrives late to a forming consensus pays more per unit of influence than the trader who moved first. This convexity is also the source of redistribution at Take: the same wedge between old and new marginal prices that makes the second unit costlier is the wedge that prevents payment from equaling return, and that drives relevance between participants.

The total influence outstanding at any moment equals exactly \(C(\mathbf{q}_m)\) — the market's current cost.

The Unmake row is the only row where every participant holds zero influence. This is not a separate outcome produced by Unmake — it is the consequence of Unmake converting each participant's influence to balance exactly. No participant retains influence in a closed market; therefore none remains. The zero is a residue of individual conservation, not an independent event.

The \(B_{\text{tot}}\) column is the mirror image of \(I_{\text{tot}}\). As influence grows, balance shrinks by exactly the same amount. As influence returns to zero after Unmake, balance recovers it entirely. The two columns are not independent — they are a single conserved quantity partitioned between two forms. \(R_{\text{tot}} = B_{\text{tot}} + I_{\text{tot}} = 4.0000\) at every row, without exception.

VIIIConservation of Relevance — Formal Proof

The preceding simulations verified conservation numerically, step by step. What follows elevates that verification to a proof — establishing that conservation holds for every possible execution of every possible operation, precisely characterizing each operation's individual and systemic effects, and giving a rigorous account of the temporal boundary that governs each taker's exposure.

The Invariant, Precisely Stated

Define total relevance as:

$$R_{\text{total}} = \sum_{i} B_i + I_{\text{total}}, \qquad I_{\text{total}} = \sum_{\text{markets}} C(\mathbf{q}_m)$$

This global definition rests on a single structural fact: for any active market, the maker holds influence \(C(\mathbf{q}_m) - \nabla C(\mathbf{q}_m) \cdot \mathbf{q}_m\), and all takers collectively hold \(\nabla C(\mathbf{q}_m) \cdot \sum_t \mathbf{q}_t\). These sum to \(C(\mathbf{q}_m)\) — exactly the market's cost.

Foundational Lemma: Holdings Sum to Market State

Claim: For any active market with state \(\mathbf{q}_m\) and taker holdings \(\{\mathbf{q}_t\}\):

$$\sum_t \mathbf{q}_t = \mathbf{q}_m$$

Proof by induction. At creation, \(\mathbf{q}_m = \mathbf{0}\) and no takers exist. Each Take adds \(\Delta\mathbf{q}\) to both \(\mathbf{q}_m\) and the executing taker's \(\mathbf{q}_t\). The sum is preserved at every step. \(\blacksquare\)

Key Corollary: Total Influence in Any Market Equals Its Cost

$$I_{\text{market}} = \left(C(\mathbf{q}_m) - \nabla C(\mathbf{q}_m) \cdot \mathbf{q}_m\right) + \sum_t \nabla C(\mathbf{q}_m) \cdot \mathbf{q}_t = C(\mathbf{q}_m) - \nabla C(\mathbf{q}_m) \cdot \mathbf{q}_m + \nabla C(\mathbf{q}_m) \cdot \mathbf{q}_m$$
Accounting Identity
$$I_{\text{market}} = C(\mathbf{q}_m)$$

Operation 1: Make — Personal in Scope and Effect

$$\Delta B_m = -L, \qquad \Delta I_m = +L, \qquad \Delta R_m = 0$$

No other participant's balance or influence is affected.

Operation 3: Unmake — Personal \(I \to B\) Conversion for All Simultaneously

Each participant's influence converts individually to balance at the uniform rate \(\nabla C(\mathbf{q}_m)\):

$$\Delta B_m = +[C(\mathbf{q}_m) - \nabla C(\mathbf{q}_m) \cdot \mathbf{q}_m], \quad \Delta I_m = -[C(\mathbf{q}_m) - \nabla C(\mathbf{q}_m) \cdot \mathbf{q}_m], \quad \Delta R_m = 0$$ $$\Delta B_t = +\nabla C(\mathbf{q}_m) \cdot \mathbf{q}_t, \quad \Delta I_t = -\nabla C(\mathbf{q}_m) \cdot \mathbf{q}_t, \quad \Delta R_t = 0$$

The primary event is \(\Delta R_i = 0\) for every \(i\). The secondary consequence is \(I_{\text{tot}} = 0\): no participant retains influence in a closed market, so none remains. No relevance crosses between participants. The corollary confirms that total balance gained equals \(C(\mathbf{q}_m)\) and total influence lost equals \(C(\mathbf{q}_m)\) — a global check that follows from individual conservation, not a separate mechanism.

The temporal decomposition of the settlement discrepancy. For taker \(t\) who acquired position \(\mathbf{q}_t\) at market state \(\mathbf{q}_{\text{old}} \to \mathbf{q}_{\text{new}} = \mathbf{q}_{\text{old}} + \mathbf{q}_t\), paying \(\Delta C = C(\mathbf{q}_{\text{new}}) - C(\mathbf{q}_{\text{old}})\), the discrepancy between payment and receipt decomposes as:

$$\nabla C(\mathbf{q}_m) \cdot \mathbf{q}_t - \Delta C = \underbrace{-\Delta C + \nabla C(\mathbf{q}_{\text{new}}) \cdot \mathbf{q}_t}_{\Delta R_t^{\text{own}}} + \underbrace{\left[\nabla C(\mathbf{q}_m) - \nabla C(\mathbf{q}_{\text{new}})\right] \cdot \mathbf{q}_t}_{\Delta R_t^{\text{subsequent}}}$$

Why past takes are excluded. Before taker \(t\) enters, their position is \(\mathbf{q}_t = \mathbf{0}\). For every Take that occurred prior to their entry:

$$\Delta R_t^{\text{past}} = \left[\nabla C(\mathbf{q}'') - \nabla C(\mathbf{q}')\right] \cdot \mathbf{0} = 0$$

A zero position is invisible to repricing. This defines the precise temporal boundary:

PeriodMarket movesEffect on \(\Delta R_t\)
Before entry\(\mathbf{q}_{\text{initial}} \to \mathbf{q}_{\text{old}}\)\(= 0\) (no position held)
Own Take\(\mathbf{q}_{\text{old}} \to \mathbf{q}_{\text{new}}\)\(= -\Delta C + \nabla C(\mathbf{q}_{\text{new}}) \cdot \mathbf{q}_t\)
After entry\(\mathbf{q}_{\text{new}} \to \mathbf{q}_m\)\(= [\nabla C(\mathbf{q}_m) - \nabla C(\mathbf{q}_{\text{new}})] \cdot \mathbf{q}_t\)

Unmake converts whatever the bounded history has produced from influence into balance, individually and exactly.

Operation 2: Take — Social in Scope and Effect

Let \(\mathbf{q} \to \mathbf{q}' = \mathbf{q} + \Delta\mathbf{q}\).

The transacting taker (prior position \(\mathbf{q}_t\)):

$$\Delta R_t = -(C(\mathbf{q}') - C(\mathbf{q})) + \nabla C(\mathbf{q}') \cdot (\mathbf{q}_t + \Delta\mathbf{q}) - \nabla C(\mathbf{q}) \cdot \mathbf{q}_t \neq 0 \text{ in general}$$

The maker (balance unchanged):

$$\Delta R_m = [C(\mathbf{q}') - \nabla C(\mathbf{q}') \cdot \mathbf{q}'] - [C(\mathbf{q}) - \nabla C(\mathbf{q}) \cdot \mathbf{q}]$$

All existing takers \(s \neq t\) (positions and balances unchanged):

$$\Delta R_s = [\nabla C(\mathbf{q}') - \nabla C(\mathbf{q})] \cdot \mathbf{q}_s$$

The source of redistribution is the strict convexity of \(C\). For any non-trivial trade:

$$\nabla C(\mathbf{q}) \cdot \Delta\mathbf{q} < C(\mathbf{q}') - C(\mathbf{q}) < \nabla C(\mathbf{q}') \cdot \Delta\mathbf{q}$$

This wedge — intrinsic to the LMSR, impossible to design away — is simultaneously what generates redistribution between participants and what produces path-dependent pricing: the same nonlinearity that causes relevance to flow at Take causes the second unit of any outcome to cost more than the first. Nevertheless:

$$\sum_i \Delta R_i = 0 \quad \checkmark$$

Constitutional Interpretation

Relevance conservation is what makes Probabilize a closed epistemic economy. No authority can inflate credibility. No participant can conjure influence from nothing. Every unit of influence held in a market was funded by a corresponding unit of balance — ultimately traceable to the \(B = 1\) endowment every citizen receives at the beginning.

Truth, in Probabilize, is scarce. And scarcity is enforced by this law.

The temporal boundary is its own constitutional principle. A taker's exposure begins at the moment they commit capital. The market's past is not their inheritance; it is simply the state they found waiting. What they earn or lose depends entirely on what happens after they arrive: their own repricing of the market, and every subsequent repricing by others. Unmake closes the ledger at whatever that bounded history has produced, converting each participant's influence individually to balance and leaving the settled distribution of balance as the sole record of what the market determined.
