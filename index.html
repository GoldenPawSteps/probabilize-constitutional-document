IBalance

Every citizen of Probabilize begins with:

Founding Endowment
$$B = 1$$

Balance is your economic capacity — the fuel you spend to participate in markets. It is not merely wealth; it is the measure of your ability to commit capital to belief. You cannot spend what you do not have. No exceptions.

IIMarkets

A market is a formal, public commitment to a question. It is the atomic unit of epistemic life in Probabilize. There are three operations: Make, Take, and Unmake.

Make — Creating a Market

A citizen who believes a question is worth resolving creates a market by specifying:

  • Outcomes: \(\omega = (\omega_1, \ldots, \omega_n)\) — a finite, exhaustive set of mutually exclusive possibilities
  • Prior probabilities: \(p = (p_1, \ldots, p_n)\), where every \(p_i > 0\) and \(\sum_i p_i = 1\)
  • Liquidity parameter: \(b > 0\) — governing how responsive prices are to new capital

The maker is not merely asking a question. They are capitalizing it. The required liquidity deposit is:

$$L = -b \cdot \ln(\min_i \, p_i)$$

This is the worst-case subsidy the maker guarantees to pay. The market may only be created if the maker can afford it:

$$B_m \geq L$$

Upon creation, the maker's balance updates:

$$B_m \leftarrow B_m - L$$

The cost function governing all market transactions is:

LMSR Cost Function
$$C(\mathbf{q}) = L + b \cdot \ln\!\left(\sum_i p_i \cdot e^{q_i / b}\right)$$

where \(\mathbf{q} = (q_1, \ldots, q_n)\) tracks the cumulative quantity vector of all positions taken. At initialization, \(\mathbf{q} = \mathbf{0}\), and:

$$C(\mathbf{0}) = L + b \cdot \ln(1) = L$$

The market opens at exactly the cost the maker paid to create it. The maker's deposit \(L\) is not merely an entry fee — it is the foundation layer of the collective pool from which all influence is drawn. Without it, the market does not exist and no taker can commit anything.

Take — Buying or Selling a Position

Any citizen except the maker may take positions in an open market. A taker selects a vector:

$$\Delta\mathbf{q} = (\Delta q_1, \ldots, \Delta q_n)$$

Each component may be positive (buying that outcome), negative (selling that outcome), or zero (no change). The trade vector is unrestricted in sign. Two constraints govern all cases.

1. Balance constraint:

$$B_t \geq \Delta C, \quad \text{where} \quad \Delta C = C(\mathbf{q}_m + \Delta\mathbf{q}) - C(\mathbf{q}_m)$$

The formula \(B_t \leftarrow B_t - \Delta C\) is the uniform expression of three financially distinct events, determined by the sign of \(\Delta C\):

CaseConditionFinancial eventBalance constraint
Market moves up\(\Delta C > 0\)Taker pays \(\Delta C\) to the marketBinds: requires \(B_t \geq \Delta C\)
Market does not move\(\Delta C = 0\)No exchange occurs — financially neutralTrivially satisfied
Market moves down\(\Delta C < 0\)Taker receives \(|\Delta C|\) from the market; balance strictly increasesTrivially satisfied

The sign of \(\Delta C\) is determined by the net effect on the market state, not by the sign of any individual component. Because \(C\) is strictly increasing in every coordinate — its gradient \(\nabla C\) is everywhere strictly positive — the sign of \(\Delta C\) follows directly from the direction of net market movement. In particular, for any pure liquidation trade (\(\Delta\mathbf{q} \leq \mathbf{0}\) with at least one strict inequality), the market state strictly decreases in at least one coordinate and increases in none, so \(\Delta C < 0\) unconditionally. Liquidation always moves the market downward. Liquidation always returns balance to the taker.

\(\Delta C = 0\) is the only case that is financially neutral in the strict sense: no balance moves between taker and market in either direction. \(\Delta C < 0\) is not neutral — it is financially beneficial: the taker's balance strictly increases, even from zero. Both cases trivially satisfy the balance constraint, but they are categorically different in their financial consequence.

In all three cases, the market state shifts — \(\mathbf{q}_m \leftarrow \mathbf{q}_m + \Delta\mathbf{q}\) — and the implied probabilities reprice. Individual powers redistribute regardless of the financial character of the trade at the collective level.

2. Nonnegative holdings:

$$\mathbf{q}_t + \Delta\mathbf{q} \geq \mathbf{0} \quad \text{(component-wise)}$$

A taker cannot hold a negative position in any outcome. This constraint is potentially binding whenever any component of \(\Delta\mathbf{q}\) is negative — independently of whether \(\Delta C\) is positive, negative, or zero.

Trade typeConstraint that bindsConstraint that does not bind

Pure sellingNonneg: \(\mathbf{q}_t + \Delta\mathbf{q} \geq \mathbf{0}\)Balance: \(\Delta C < 0\) is always affordable
Mixed (\(\Delta C > 0\))Both may bind—
Mixed (\(\Delta C \leq 0\))Nonneg on sold componentsBalance: trivially satisfied

This establishes a constitutional asymmetry between the two constraints. The balance constraint governs buying power — economic capacity limits how much new influence a taker can acquire. The nonneg constraint governs selling power — prior commitment limits how much balance a taker can recover. A taker with \(B_t = 0\) can execute any trade with \(\Delta C \leq 0\), provided they hold positions to sell. When \(\Delta C = 0\), they leave with their balance unchanged. When \(\Delta C < 0\), they leave richer. The maximum recoverable balance from full liquidation of position \(\mathbf{q}_t\) is:

$$|\Delta C_{\text{full liquidation}}| = C(\mathbf{q}_m) - C(\mathbf{q}_m - \mathbf{q}_t) > 0$$

This is the taker's instrument for converting influence back to balance while the market remains open — available at any time, constrained only by what they hold.

Upon a valid take, the state updates:

$$\mathbf{q}_m \leftarrow \mathbf{q}_m + \Delta\mathbf{q}$$ $$\mathbf{q}_t \leftarrow \mathbf{q}_t + \Delta\mathbf{q}$$ $$B_t \leftarrow B_t - \Delta C$$

The No-Shorting Principle

The nonneg holdings constraint is not merely a technical restriction. It is a constitutional one.

You cannot express a negative belief. You can only reduce a positive one.

A taker who holds no position in outcome \(i\) cannot sell \(\omega_i\). There is no mechanism in Probabilize for acquiring influence through a claim that something will not happen, absent a prior position purchased at cost. This principle has its full grounding in the backing structure of the collective pool: influence is a positive claim on a positive pool. The pool has no negative layer. You cannot hold negative influence not merely because you haven't paid for it yourself, but because there is no pool entry that corresponds to a negative commitment.

Unmake — Closing a Market

Only the maker may close a market. Upon unmake, all positions are liquidated and capital is distributed according to the market-implied probabilities at the time of closure.

The gradient of \(C\) is:

$$\frac{\partial C}{\partial q_i} = \frac{p_i \cdot e^{q_i / b}}{\sum_j p_j \cdot e^{q_j / b}}$$

These gradient components sum to 1 and represent the market-implied probability of each outcome — this is how the market speaks.

The maker receives:

$$B_m \leftarrow B_m + C(\mathbf{q}_m) - \nabla C(\mathbf{q}_m) \cdot \mathbf{q}_m$$

Each taker receives:

$$B_t \leftarrow B_t + \nabla C(\mathbf{q}_m) \cdot \mathbf{q}_t$$
Unmake is settlement at current prices, not reimbursement at cost. What each participant receives is not what they originally paid. The operation converts each participant's influence to balance individually and simultaneously, at the uniform rate \(\nabla C(\mathbf{q}_m)\). For every participant, \(\Delta B_i = +I_i\) and \(\Delta I_i = -I_i\), so \(\Delta P_i = 0\) exactly. The total influence across the market reaches zero as a consequence of these individual conversions — because no participant retains any influence in a closed market, and no influence is left over. This is not retirement or destruction; it is the residue of perfect individual conservation applied simultaneously to all.

Unmake is the maker's instrument for collective dissolution of a market. Liquidation — partial or complete selling of a taker's position while the market remains open — is the taker's instrument for individual recovery. Both convert influence to balance, but through categorically different mechanisms: Unmake closes the ledger for everyone at once; liquidation adjusts one taker's position while the market continues.

A taker's net power gain or loss is determined entirely by what happens from the moment they take a position forward — their own trades, and every subsequent trade that reprices their stake. The market's past is not their inheritance.

The market is settled and ceases to exist.

IIIInfluence

While a market is active, capital is not sitting idle in balances — it is deployed into the market structure. Influence measures the value of your deployed capital across all open markets.

For a maker of market \(m\):

$$I_m^{\text{maker}} = C(\mathbf{q}_m) - \nabla C(\mathbf{q}_m) \cdot \mathbf{q}_m$$

For a taker in market \(m\):

$$I_t^{\text{taker}} = \nabla C(\mathbf{q}_m) \cdot \mathbf{q}_t$$

The total influence outstanding across all participants in a market equals \(C(\mathbf{q}_m)\) — the current cost of the market — which is itself the sum of all committed balance since creation:

$$C(\mathbf{q}_m) = \underbrace{L}_{\text{maker's deposit}} + \underbrace{\sum_k \Delta C^{(k)}}_{\text{all takers' cumulative net payments}}$$

This identity holds because \(C(\mathbf{0}) = L\) and each Take changes \(C\) by exactly \(\Delta C^{(k)}\), whether positive, negative, or zero. Every individual influence claim is therefore a share of this collective pool, not a claim backed by that participant's payment alone. The pool is what makes each position meaningful.

Total influence is the sum across all active markets in which a person participates. Influence is dynamic — it fluctuates as others take positions and shift the implied probabilities. A taker whose outcome becomes more probable sees their influence rise. A taker on a less favored outcome sees it fall. This fluctuation is not noise. It is the continuous repricing of committed belief by the market, and it begins the moment a position is acquired — not before.

IVPower — The Conserved Quantity

The most fundamental law in Probabilize is the conservation of Power:

Conservation Law
$$P = B + I$$

Power is the sum of balance and influence. To state the conservation law with full precision requires attending to two distinct layers: the collective layer, where the law is exact and mechanical, and the individual layer, where it permits asymmetry.

The Collective Layer: A Fundamental Relation

For every operation, without exception:

Collective Conservation
$$\Delta I_{\text{tot}} = -\Delta B_{\text{tot}}$$

Influence is not created from nothing. Every unit of influence that enters the system displaces exactly one unit of collective balance. Every unit of influence that leaves the system restores exactly one unit of collective balance. The two columns are not merely correlated — they are a single conserved quantity partitioned between two forms.

This holds for all three operations, and for Take in all its cases:

Make: Only the maker's balance moves. The market acquires \(C(\mathbf{0}) = L\) of influence.

$$\Delta B_{\text{tot}} = -L, \qquad \Delta I_{\text{tot}} = +L, \qquad \Delta B_{\text{tot}} + \Delta I_{\text{tot}} = 0 \checkmark$$

Take (\(\Delta C > 0\)): The taker pays. Collective influence rises.

$$\Delta B_{\text{tot}} = -\Delta C, \qquad \Delta I_{\text{tot}} = +\Delta C, \qquad \Delta B_{\text{tot}} + \Delta I_{\text{tot}} = 0 \checkmark$$

Take (\(\Delta C = 0\)): No financial exchange. Collective quantities are unchanged. This is the only case that is financially neutral at the collective level — no balance moves in either direction — yet the market state shifts and individual powers redistribute through repricing alone.

$$\Delta B_{\text{tot}} = 0, \qquad \Delta I_{\text{tot}} = 0, \qquad \Delta B_{\text{tot}} + \Delta I_{\text{tot}} = 0 \checkmark$$

Take (\(\Delta C < 0\)): The taker receives. Collective influence falls. This case is not neutral — it is financially beneficial to the taker. Because \(\nabla C > 0\) everywhere, any pure liquidation trade unconditionally produces \(\Delta C < 0\).

$$\Delta B_{\text{tot}} = -\Delta C > 0, \qquad \Delta I_{\text{tot}} = +\Delta C < 0, \qquad \Delta B_{\text{tot}} + \Delta I_{\text{tot}} = 0 \checkmark$$

Unmake: Every participant's influence converts to balance. Collective balance recovers \(C(\mathbf{q}_m)\) exactly.

$$\Delta B_{\text{tot}} = +C(\mathbf{q}_m), \qquad \Delta I_{\text{tot}} = -C(\mathbf{q}_m), \qquad \Delta B_{\text{tot}} + \Delta I_{\text{tot}} = 0 \checkmark$$

The Individual Layer: Where the Constitution Permits Asymmetry

The collective exchange is exact and invariant. The individual layer is where asymmetry enters — but only through Take.

Make is personal in both scope and effect. Only the maker is involved. The maker pays \(L\) from balance and gains exactly \(L\) in influence. No other participant exists in this operation.

$$\Delta P_m = 0 \quad \text{(maker only; others untouched)}$$

Unmake is social in scope but personal in effect. The maker initiates it unilaterally, but it converts the influence of every participant simultaneously — each individually, at the uniform rate \(\nabla C(\mathbf{q}_m)\). For every participant \(i\): \(\Delta B_i = +I_i\), \(\Delta I_i = -I_i\), \(\Delta P_i = 0\). No power moves between participants. The total influence across the market reaches zero as a consequence of these individual conversions — not as an independent event, but as what remains when every participant's influence has been exactly converted to balance and nothing is left to hold.

This personal character of Unmake has a precise temporal basis. Before a taker enters the market, their position is \(\mathbf{q}_t = \mathbf{0}\). Every Take that occurred before their entry shifted \(\nabla C\) — but applied to a zero position, all such shifts leave the pre-entry taker completely unaffected. Exposure begins at the moment they commit capital and ends at Unmake. The market's prior history is constitutionally irrelevant to their settlement.

Take is social in both scope and effect, regardless of trade direction or cost. Every Take — whether \(\Delta C\) is positive, zero, or negative — reprices the holdings of every participant in the market simultaneously. The taker funds or receives the entire change in collective influence unilaterally. How the redistribution lands at the individual level is determined by the convexity of the cost function.

$$\Delta P_i \neq 0 \text{ in general}, \quad \text{but} \quad \sum_i \Delta P_i = 0 \quad \text{always}$$

The lifecycle of a position makes this concrete:

$$\underbrace{B_t \leftarrow B_t - \Delta C}_{\substack{\text{pay if } \Delta C > 0 \\ \text{no exchange if } \Delta C = 0 \\ \text{receive if } \Delta C < 0}} \;\longrightarrow\; \underbrace{\text{Hold } \mathbf{q}_t}_{\substack{\text{influence fluctuates} \\ \text{from entry forward}}} \;\longrightarrow\; \underbrace{\text{Receive } \nabla C(\mathbf{q}_m) \cdot \mathbf{q}_t}_{\text{Unmake: influence converted individually}}$$
Operation\(\Delta B_{\text{tot}} + \Delta I_{\text{tot}}\)Individual \(\Delta P_i\)System \(\Delta P\)Mechanism
Make\(= 0\)\(0\) for maker; others untouched\(0\)Personal \(B \to I\) conversion

Take is the only operation where power crosses between participants. Make creates the arena. Unmake dissolves it. Take is where the actual epistemic and economic contest occurs.

No position exists without the maker's foundation, and no taker's influence is backed by their own payment alone. Every claim is a share of the pool, repriced continuously as the market evolves. The no-shorting principle has its full grounding here: influence is a positive claim on a positive pool. The pool has no negative layer. You cannot hold negative influence because the collective backing structure admits no such entry.

Influence in Probabilize is not an abstract score. It is balance that has been committed to a market — transformed in form, not in magnitude, and recoverable either through Unmake (the maker's collective instrument) or through voluntary liquidation (the taker's individual instrument, available whenever the market is open). The scarcity of influence is the scarcity of balance. And balance is scarce because every citizen begins with exactly \(B = 1\), and no operation creates it from nothing.

VGovernance

Markets in Probabilize are not merely predictive instruments. They are governing mechanisms.

Two properties determine a market's governance role:

Market-implied probability — the gradient \(\nabla C(\mathbf{q}_m)\) — defines the probability distribution over outcomes as collectively expressed by all committed capital. This is not a poll. It is not a vote. It is a distribution weighted by skin in the game.

Credibility — \(C(\mathbf{q}_m)\), the current cost of the market — measures how much total capital backs this distribution. A market with higher cost has attracted more committed belief. It speaks with greater authority.

When a governance decision is required:

The outcome is sampled randomly according to \(\nabla C(\mathbf{q}_m)\), and its authority is proportional to \(C(\mathbf{q}_m)\).

This is the core epistemology of Probabilize: truth is not declared, it is sampled from a distribution of committed capital. No individual can dictate the outcome. No authority overrides the market. The system governs through probabilistic aggregation of genuine belief.

VIThe Mechanics in Motion — A Worked Example

Abstract principles require concrete instantiation. What follows is a full worked example — every formula evaluated, every balance tracked, every unit of power accounted for — demonstrating that the constitutional guarantees are not aspirational but arithmetically necessary.

The Scenario

A binary market is opened on the question: "Does the proposal pass?"

ParameterValue
Outcomes\(\omega_1 =\) "Yes", \(\omega_2 =\) "No"
Prior\(p = (0.60,\ 0.40)\)
Liquidity\(b = 0.5\)
MakerAlice, \(B_A = 1.0000\)
Taker 1Bob, \(B_B = 1.0000\)
Taker 2Carol, \(B_C = 1.0000\)

Three citizens. Three units of power in the system. That total will not change.

Step 1

Alice Makes the Market

The liquidity requirement is:

$$L = -b \cdot \ln(\min_i\, p_i) = -0.5 \cdot \ln(0.40) = -0.5 \cdot (-0.9163) = \mathbf{0.4581}$$

\(L\) is the maker's maximum possible loss: it grows with \(b\) (deeper liquidity demands more capital) and with the "surprise" of the rarest outcome. At \(b \to 0\), markets become infinitely sharp and cheap; at \(b \to \infty\), infinitely liquid and expensive.

Alice can afford this — \(B_A = 1.0000 \geq 0.4581\) — and the market is created:

$$B_A \leftarrow 1.0000 - 0.4581 = \mathbf{0.5419}$$

The market initializes at \(\mathbf{q} = (0, 0)\), and the cost function confirms it opened at exactly \(L\):

$$C(0,0) = 0.4581 + 0.5 \cdot \ln\!\left(0.6 \cdot e^0 + 0.4 \cdot e^0\right) = 0.4581 + 0.5 \cdot \ln(1) = \mathbf{0.4581}$$

Make is personal in both scope and effect: \(\Delta B_A = -L\), \(\Delta I_A = +L\), \(\Delta P_A = 0\). Alice's deposit of \(0.4581\) is the foundation layer of the collective pool — the precondition for any subsequent trading. Bob and Carol do not exist within this operation.

Cost at the new state:

Step 3

Carol Takes a Position on "No"

Carol acquires 1 unit of \(\omega_2\), committing \(\Delta\mathbf{q} = (0, 1)\) against the current market state \(\mathbf{q}_m = (1, 0)\). The new state becomes \(\mathbf{q}_m = (1, 1)\), and Carol's holdings are \(\mathbf{q}_C = (0, 1)\).

Cost at the new state:

Carol's trade cost: \(\Delta C_C = 1.4581 - 1.2458 = \mathbf{0.2123}\)

Pure buying (\(\Delta C > 0\)): Carol pays \(0.2123\) to the market. The pool now totals \(C(1,1) = 1.4581 = 0.4581 + 0.7877 + 0.2123\). Carol entered at state \((1,0)\). The market's prior history, including Bob's distortion to 91.73%, is not her inheritance. She paid \(0.2123\) for a position immediately worth \(\nabla C(1,1) \cdot (0,1) = 0.4000\): her own-Take drift is \(+0.1877\), sourced from Bob's share of the pool shrinking as the gradient was restored.

$$B_C \leftarrow 1.0000 - 0.2123 = \mathbf{0.7877}$$

Implied probabilities after Carol's trade:

$$\pi_1 = \frac{0.6 \cdot e^2}{e^2} = \mathbf{0.6000}, \qquad \pi_2 = \frac{0.4 \cdot e^2}{e^2} = \mathbf{0.4000}$$

When both outcome positions are equal, the exponential terms cancel and implied probabilities revert exactly to the priors. Carol's countervailing trade has restored the prior distribution — but at different prices than those at which Bob acted. The market has memory.

Step 4

Alice Unmakes the Market

Alice closes the market at final state \(\mathbf{q}_m = (1,1)\), \(\nabla C = (0.6000,\ 0.4000)\). Each participant's share of the collective pool is converted individually to balance at this uniform rate: \(\Delta B_i = +I_i\), \(\Delta I_i = -I_i\), \(\Delta P_i = 0\) for every \(i\).

$$B_A \leftarrow 0.5419 + \left[1.4581 - (0.6 + 0.4)\right] = 0.5419 + 0.4581 = \mathbf{1.0000}$$ $$B_B \leftarrow 0.2123 + 0.6000 = \mathbf{0.8123}$$ $$B_C \leftarrow 0.7877 + 0.4000 = \mathbf{1.1877}$$

The entire pool of \(1.4581\) is distributed. The entire redistribution was determined before Unmake ran. Unmake converts; it does not redistribute.

$$\text{Total: } 1.0000 + 0.8123 + 1.1877 = \mathbf{3.0000} \checkmark$$

VIIExtended Simulation — The Infrastructure Bill Market

VIIIConservation of Power — Formal Proof

The preceding simulations verified conservation numerically, step by step. What follows elevates that verification to a proof — establishing that conservation holds for every possible execution of every possible operation, precisely characterizing each operation's individual and systemic effects, giving a rigorous account of the temporal boundary that governs each taker's exposure, and proving that liquidation unconditionally produces \(\Delta C < 0\).

The Invariant, Precisely Stated

Define total power as:

$$P_{\text{total}} = \sum_{i} B_i + I_{\text{total}}, \qquad I_{\text{total}} = \sum_{\text{markets}} C(\mathbf{q}_m)$$

For any active market, \(C(\mathbf{q}_m) = L + \sum_k \Delta C^{(k)}\) — the maker's foundation plus all incremental taker net payments. The maker holds influence \(C(\mathbf{q}_m) - \nabla C(\mathbf{q}_m) \cdot \mathbf{q}_m\), and all takers collectively hold \(\nabla C(\mathbf{q}_m) \cdot \sum_t \mathbf{q}_t\). These sum to \(C(\mathbf{q}_m)\).

Foundational Lemma: Holdings Sum to Market State

Claim: For any active market with state \(\mathbf{q}_m\) and taker holdings \(\{\mathbf{q}_t\}\):

$$\sum_t \mathbf{q}_t = \mathbf{q}_m$$

Proof by induction. At creation, \(\mathbf{q}_m = \mathbf{0}\) and no takers exist. Each Take adds \(\Delta\mathbf{q}\) to both \(\mathbf{q}_m\) and the executing taker's \(\mathbf{q}_t\). The sum is preserved at every step, regardless of the sign of \(\Delta\mathbf{q}\) or the value of \(\Delta C\). \(\blacksquare\)

Key Corollary: Total Influence in Any Market Equals Its Cost

$$I_{\text{market}} = \left(C(\mathbf{q}_m) - \nabla C(\mathbf{q}_m) \cdot \mathbf{q}_m\right) + \sum_t \nabla C(\mathbf{q}_m) \cdot \mathbf{q}_t = C(\mathbf{q}_m) - \nabla C(\mathbf{q}_m) \cdot \mathbf{q}_m + \nabla C(\mathbf{q}_m) \cdot \mathbf{q}_m$$
Accounting Identity
$$I_{\text{market}} = C(\mathbf{q}_m)$$

Structural Lemma: Liquidation Always Moves the Market Downward

Claim: For any pure liquidation trade — \(\Delta\mathbf{q} \leq \mathbf{0}\) with at least one strict inequality — we have \(\Delta C < 0\) unconditionally.

Proof. The gradient of \(C\) is strictly positive in every component at every market state:

$$\frac{\partial C}{\partial q_i} = \frac{p_i \cdot e^{q_i/b}}{\sum_j p_j \cdot e^{q_j/b}} > 0 \quad \text{for all } i,\ \text{all } \mathbf{q}$$

since \(p_i > 0\) and all exponentials are positive. Therefore \(C\) is strictly increasing in every coordinate. For any \(\Delta\mathbf{q} \leq \mathbf{0}\) with at least one strict inequality, the market state \(\mathbf{q}_m + \Delta\mathbf{q}\) is strictly less than \(\mathbf{q}_m\) in at least one coordinate and no greater in any. By strict monotonicity:

$$C(\mathbf{q}_m + \Delta\mathbf{q}) < C(\mathbf{q}_m) \implies \Delta C < 0 \quad \blacksquare$$

This is not a special case or regime. It is an unconditional consequence of \(\nabla C > 0\) everywhere. Liquidation always returns balance to the taker. The balance constraint never binds on a pure liquidation trade. The only binding constraint is the nonneg holdings constraint: you cannot sell what you do not hold. The maximum balance recoverable from full liquidation of position \(\mathbf{q}_t\) is:

$$|\Delta C_{\text{full liquidation}}| = C(\mathbf{q}_m) - C(\mathbf{q}_m - \mathbf{q}_t) > 0$$

The strictly positive gradient also establishes the dual asymmetry governing Take: convexity makes buying increasingly expensive (path-dependent pricing), while strict positivity makes selling always remunerative (liquidation always beneficial).

Operation 1: Make — Foundation Layer and Personal \(B \to I\) Conversion

$$\Delta B_m = -L, \qquad \Delta I_m = +L, \qquad \Delta P_m = 0$$ $$\Delta B_{\text{tot}} = -L, \qquad \Delta I_{\text{tot}} = +L, \qquad \Delta B_{\text{tot}} + \Delta I_{\text{tot}} = 0$$

The maker's deposit establishes the pool at \(C(\mathbf{0}) = L\). No other participant's balance or influence is affected.

Operation 3: Unmake — Personal \(I \to B\) Conversion for All Simultaneously

Each participant's share of the collective pool converts individually to balance at the uniform rate \(\nabla C(\mathbf{q}_m)\):

$$\Delta B_m = +[C(\mathbf{q}_m) - \nabla C(\mathbf{q}_m) \cdot \mathbf{q}_m], \quad \Delta I_m = -[C(\mathbf{q}_m) - \nabla C(\mathbf{q}_m) \cdot \mathbf{q}_m], \quad \Delta P_m = 0$$ $$\Delta B_t = +\nabla C(\mathbf{q}_m) \cdot \mathbf{q}_t, \quad \Delta I_t = -\nabla C(\mathbf{q}_m) \cdot \mathbf{q}_t, \quad \Delta P_t = 0$$

The primary event is \(\Delta P_i = 0\) for every \(i\). Summing by the corollary:

$$\Delta B_{\text{tot}} = +C(\mathbf{q}_m), \qquad \Delta I_{\text{tot}} = -C(\mathbf{q}_m), \qquad \Delta B_{\text{tot}} + \Delta I_{\text{tot}} = 0$$

The total influence reaches zero as the consequence of individual conservation. The entire pool is distributed.

The temporal decomposition of the settlement discrepancy. For taker \(t\) who accumulated net position \(\mathbf{q}_t\), the discrepancy between net payment and receipt at Unmake decomposes as:

$$\nabla C(\mathbf{q}_m) \cdot \mathbf{q}_t - \Delta C_t^{\text{net}} = \underbrace{-\Delta C_t^{\text{net}} + \nabla C(\mathbf{q}_{\text{entry}}) \cdot \mathbf{q}_t}_{\Delta P_t^{\text{own}}} + \underbrace{\left[\nabla C(\mathbf{q}_m) - \nabla C(\mathbf{q}_{\text{entry}})\right] \cdot \mathbf{q}_t}_{\Delta P_t^{\text{subsequent}}}$$

Why past takes are excluded. Before taker \(t\) holds any position, \(\mathbf{q}_t = \mathbf{0}\). For every market movement prior to their first purchase:

$$\Delta P_t^{\text{past}} = \left[\nabla C(\mathbf{q}'') - \nabla C(\mathbf{q}')\right] \cdot \mathbf{0} = 0$$

A zero position is invisible to repricing. This defines the precise temporal boundary:

Operation 2: Take — Collective Exchange and Individual Redistribution

Let \(\mathbf{q} \to \mathbf{q}' = \mathbf{q} + \Delta\mathbf{q}\), where \(\Delta\mathbf{q}\) satisfies the nonneg holdings constraint.

At the collective layer — valid for all signs of \(\Delta C\):

$$\Delta B_{\text{tot}} = -\Delta C, \qquad \Delta I_{\text{tot}} = +\Delta C, \qquad \Delta B_{\text{tot}} + \Delta I_{\text{tot}} = 0$$

Only the taker's balance moves. When \(\Delta C > 0\), the taker pays and collective influence rises. When \(\Delta C = 0\), no balance transfers — the only case that is financially neutral at the collective level — yet the market state shifts and individual powers redistribute. When \(\Delta C < 0\) (guaranteed for any pure liquidation by the structural lemma), the taker receives and their balance strictly increases.

At the individual layer:

The transacting taker (prior position \(\mathbf{q}_t\)):

$$\Delta P_t = -(C(\mathbf{q}') - C(\mathbf{q})) + \nabla C(\mathbf{q}') \cdot (\mathbf{q}_t + \Delta\mathbf{q}) - \nabla C(\mathbf{q}) \cdot \mathbf{q}_t \neq 0 \text{ in general}$$

The maker (balance unchanged):

$$\Delta P_m = [C(\mathbf{q}') - \nabla C(\mathbf{q}') \cdot \mathbf{q}'] - [C(\mathbf{q}) - \nabla C(\mathbf{q}) \cdot \mathbf{q}]$$

All existing takers \(s \neq t\) (positions and balances unchanged):

$$\Delta P_s = [\nabla C(\mathbf{q}') - \nabla C(\mathbf{q})] \cdot \mathbf{q}_s$$

The source of redistribution is the strict convexity of \(C\). For non-trivial trades with \(\Delta C \neq 0\):

$$\nabla C(\mathbf{q}) \cdot \Delta\mathbf{q} < C(\mathbf{q}') - C(\mathbf{q}) < \nabla C(\mathbf{q}') \cdot \Delta\mathbf{q}$$

For zero-cost trades (\(\Delta C = 0\)), redistribution still occurs through the repricing of existing positions. In all cases:

$$\sum_i \Delta P_i = 0 \quad \checkmark$$

Constitutional Interpretation

Power conservation is what makes Probabilize a closed epistemic economy. No authority can inflate credibility. No participant can conjure influence from nothing. Every unit of influence held in a market is backed by the collective pool of committed balance — the maker's foundation deposit and all takers' cumulative net payments together — ultimately traceable to the \(B = 1\) endowment every citizen receives at the beginning.

No position exists without the maker's foundation, and no taker's influence is backed by their own payment alone. Every claim is a share of the collective pool, repriced continuously as the market evolves.
Truth, in Probabilize, is scarce. And scarcity is enforced by this law.

The two constraints on Take enforce two categorically different forms of scarcity, grounded in two dual properties of \(C\). Strict convexity makes buying increasingly expensive — the further the market has already moved, the more each additional unit costs. Strictly positive gradient makes selling always remunerative — every liquidation unconditionally returns balance to the taker, with the nonneg constraint as the only limit. Economic capacity limits how much influence you can acquire. Prior commitment limits how much balance you can recover. These are not symmetric: one is a limit on what you can build, the other is a guarantee of what you can reclaim.
